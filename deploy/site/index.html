What we do

Real local journalism, powered by AI, verified by a human editor

Town government produces more public record than any journalist can cover alone — hundreds of board and committee meetings each year, each with agendas, packets, and video. TownBrief closes that gap. We record every public meeting, transcribe it locally, and generate AP-style articles and deep reads that a human editor reviews before publication.

The result is more coverage, faster — at a fraction of traditional newsroom cost — without sacrificing accuracy or the civic accountability that local journalism exists to provide.

📹

Every meeting, on record

We download and archive government meeting videos from public sources and cable-access stations.

🎤

Word-for-word transcripts

Audio is transcribed locally using state-of-the-art speech recognition — no third-party data sharing.

📄

Articles grounded in documents

Agendas, packets, and minutes from town portals are fetched and used to verify article content.

Our story

How TownBrief began

Like most towns, Wayland, Massachusetts had dedicated boards and committees that met regularly, kept meticulous records, and made decisions that shaped daily life — and almost none of it made the news. The MetroWest Daily News had cut its local staff. The legacy weeklies were gone. Town meeting votes, school budget debates, conservation hearings — they happened, were recorded, and vanished.

TownBrief started from a simple observation: the public record already exists. Meeting videos are online. Agendas are posted. Minutes are published. What was missing was a journalist with the time to turn all of it into readable coverage.

Early 2025
The pipeline begins. A local journalist builds an automated system to download WayCAM meeting videos for Wayland, transcribe them locally, and draft AP-style articles — with a human editor reviewing every piece before publication.
Late 2025
The Wayland Post launches. Wayland's first dedicated digital outlet publishes government coverage, daily roundups, sports, and obituaries — covering more committees than any prior outlet, every week.
June 2026
Sudbury joins the network. The pipeline extends to a second town and the TownBrief fleet model is established — one publication per town, shared infrastructure, each with its own editorial identity.
June 2026
Middlesex County News goes live. A county-wide aggregator pulls the most important stories across the network into a single daily read for readers who follow multiple towns.
2026 – ongoing
The data layer opens. TownBrief's local government corpus — transcripts, articles, agendas — becomes available to developers and AI tools via API and MCP, turning every meeting into a queryable record.
Data & API

Local government intelligence for AI and developers

TownBrief's pipeline produces a structured, timestamped corpus of local government activity — every transcript, article, agenda, and vote — accessible via REST API and as a Model Context Protocol (MCP) server. Wire it into your AI assistant, civic app, or research tool.

Ask any AI assistant connected to TownBrief's MCP:

# Example tool calls search_meetings("Wayland", committee="Planning Board", query="40B housing") get_article(meeting_id="wayland-select-board-2026-06-10") get_committee_summary("Sudbury", committee="Finance Committee", since="2026-01-01") list_recent_meetings("Wayland", limit=10)
search_meetings()
Full-text search across all transcripts and articles by town, committee, date range, or keyword.
get_transcript()
Full transcript text and timestamped SRT for any meeting in the archive.
get_article()
AP-style article, brief summary, and headline candidates for any meeting.
get_committee_summary()
Rolled-up activity digest for a committee over a date range — votes, topics, attendees.
